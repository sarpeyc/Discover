Finland

Photo credits - © unknown

Finland is a country located in the north of Europe with a popultion of 5.53 million and with a national language of Finnish. Finland is known for nature's most spectacular light show, the Aurora Borealis,commonly known as the Northern Lights. It is in the centre of the "Aurora Zone" which means any further north, or any further south, the chances of seeing the Northern Lights aren’t as good. It's position being inland also reduces the chances of coming across coastal fog that would obstruct the veiw of the Northern Light.

Norway

Norway

Photo credits - © Ingimage

Norway is a Nordic country located in Northern Europe with a population of 5.3 million. Norway is known for its Fjords which is a deep, narrow body of water that reaches inland. The fjords were formed by the giant glacier tongues that through several ice ages have shaped the landscape. A fjord is thus a U-shaped undersea valley, and on the west coast, this valley is often surrounded by dramatic mountain scenery. Norway holds around 1000 fjords in which 10 that are accessed by cruise ships regularly.

Portugal

Portugal

Photo credits - © Dale Johnson

Portugal is a country located in the southernwest of Europe and is bordered by the Atlantic Ocean and the east of Spain. Portugal’s climate is mainly Mediterranean. The southern regions are dry and sunny with warm/hot summers and mild/rainy winters. Traveling north, the weather pattern becomes cooler and wetter, especially in winter. Snow is possible in the mountains of the northeast.
