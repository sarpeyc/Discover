California

Photo credits - © National Geographic

California is a state in the Pacific Region of the United States. With a population of 39.5 million it is the most popular state in the US and the third largest by size. The San Francisco Bay Area and the Greater Los Angeles Area are seen as the originators of the global technology and entertainment industries. Although California is well known for its warm climate, the large size of the state means that the climates can vary from moist, humid rainforest-like climate in the north to hot desert-like climates in the centre.

Florida

Florida

Photo credits - © Getty

Florida is a state located in the southeastern region of the United States with a population of 21 million. Florida has the longest coatline in the US. The city of Miami is known for its Latin-American cultural influences and notable arts scene, as well as its nightlife, especially in upscale South Beach. Orlando is famed for theme parks, including Walt Disney World.

Mexico

Mexico

Photo credits - © Marc Filion

Mexico is a country found in the southern region of America. It is the 13th largest country in the world with a population of approximately 128 million. Mexico is a federation holding 31 states and Mexico City being it's capital state and also it's largest state. The state of Yucatán hosts one of the most beautiful underwater caves. These caves are known to connect with other cave systems and can be explored for 100 kilometers or more. There are more than 3,000 underwater caves in the state of Yucatán and only 1,400 of them having been studied and recorded.

Oregon

Oregon

Photo credits - © Getty

Oregon is a state found in the pacific northwest on the West Coast of the United States. Before European traders, explorers, and settlers arrived Oregon was populated by many indigenous tribes. Oregon is known to be one of the most geographically diverse states in the U.S consisting of volcanoes, abundant bodies of water, greenery and mixed forests, as well as deserts and shrublands. The lake shown in the photo is Crater Lake which is the ddepst lake in the country and very well known for it's clear, deep blue waters. The lake sits in Oregon’s only national park, the Crater Lake National Park, which was created over 7,500 years ago when the top of Mount Mazama collapsed.

Wyoming

Wyoming

Photo credits - © Getty

Wyoming is a state found in the western region of the United States with a population of 578,759. It is bordered on the north by Montana, on the east by South Dakota and Nebraska, on the south by Colorado, on the southwest by Utah, and on the west by Idaho and Montana. Wyoming is home to the first and oldest National Park, Yellowstone. The park is known for its wildlife and its many geothermal features, especially Old Faithful geyser, one of its most popular.
